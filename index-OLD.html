Investor brief · Bay of Plenty, NZ

The orchard runs on diesel.
We're changing the fuel.

Voltage owns, charges, maintains and delivers a fleet of electric orchard machinery — tractors, forklifts, UTVs, excavators — plus on-orchard solar and batteries, leased to kiwifruit growers as one monthly bill. No capex for the grower. No diesel. A 48-hour swap guarantee.

Why now

A concentrated market, ready to switch

The entire opportunity sits within one hour of a single depot — a customer density no other horticultural region on earth offers an electrification business.

Demand

Growers rent anyway

Every orchard needs a forklift for six weeks of harvest and owns one for fifty-two. Seasonal machinery demand is already met by hire — just with diesel, and without a service guarantee.

Pressure

Carbon reporting is coming to the orchard gate

The industry's sustainability commitments flow down to growers as emissions accounting. A leased electric fleet is the easiest line item a grower can convert — with auditable numbers we hand them.

Moat

We carry the risk they won't

Growers don't trust the residual value or parts supply of imported electric machinery. We own both problems — direct-import sourcing, deep spares inventory, and a 48-hour swap that makes reliability our job, not theirs.

The model

Three engines, one client relationship

Each layer de-risks the next: hire gets us in the gate, leases build the recurring base, and the energy package locks the relationship in for fifteen years.

Machines · Short-term hire

Seasonal fleet

Electric forklifts, excavators and bin-running gear, delivered for the harvest peak and development work.

  • Proven battery duty cycles — zero technology risk
  • Counter-seasonal to the lease fleet
  • Beachhead product: profitable from year one
Machines · Long-term lease

Everyday fleet

Utility tractors and UTVs on 12-month all-inclusive leases: maintenance, insurance, spares and swap included.

  • Recurring, contracted revenue
  • 48-hour replacement guarantee
  • Charges on-orchard — from engine 03
Energy · 15-year lease

Orchard energy package

Right-sized solar, inverter and battery, installed by certified partners, owned and billed by us.

  • Fleet charging makes the solar pay
  • Battery-backed frost and irrigation pumps
  • One invoice for machines and energy

Roadmap

Prove it small, scale it funded

The business has a scale threshold — overhead amortises across the fleet. We reach it in tranches, each one raised against signed contracts, not projections.

Phase 1 · This harvest

Paid pilot

Four electric forklifts and two tractors with founding growers. A full Bay of Plenty season of reliability, duty-cycle and cost data — and signed letters of intent.

~$350k capital
Phase 2 · Year 1–2

Fleet + depot

Depot with solar and V2G-capable charging, delivery truck, first technician. Lease fleet deployed against LOIs; energy packages offered to every machine client.

~$4–5M capital
Phase 3 · Year 3+

The electrification layer

Idle fleet becomes grid storage. Energy book compounds. Expansion to adjacent horticulture — avocados, apples — on the same playbook.

$8M+ deployed
